# Test Symptoms for Different Risk Levels

## Low Risk (10-30%)
- "mild headache"
- "slight cough"
- "minor cut on finger"
- "feeling a bit tired"
- "mild stomach discomfort"

## Low-Moderate Risk (30-45%)
- "moderate headache that's been going on for a few days"
- "fever and body aches"
- "stomach ache after eating"
- "persistent cough"
- "feeling unwell"

## Moderate Risk (45-60%)
- "significant bleeding from a cut that won't stop" ✅ (should be ~52%)
- "moderate abdominal pain that's getting worse"
- "persistent fever over 101"
- "worsening pain in my side"
- "moderate chest discomfort"

## Moderate-High Risk (60-75%)
- "severe headache with nausea"
- "moderate injury to wrist with swelling"
- "abdominal pain with vomiting"
- "persistent fever with severe fatigue"
- "significant pain that's getting worse"

## High Risk (75-90%)
- "broken arm"
- "dislocated shoulder"
- "severe abdominal pain"
- "moderate chest pain"
- "fractured wrist"

## Critical/Urgent (90-95%)
- "im dying"
- "heart is hurting"
- "chest pain and shortness of breath"
- "my heart is hurting. im bleeding"
- "broken arm and dislocated foot"
- "severe chest pain with difficulty breathing"

## Test These Specific Ones:

1. **"moderate abdominal pain that's getting worse"** → Should be ~45-55%
2. **"severe headache with nausea"** → Should be ~65-75%
3. **"broken arm"** → Should be ~80-88%
4. **"mild headache"** → Should be ~15-25%
5. **"chest pain and shortness of breath"** → Should be ~90% (critical)
